Buffon’s Needle

An interactive geometric probability experiment

Page 1 — Drop Many Needles

Parallel lines are drawn on the floor, with distance D between neighboring lines. A needle of length L is dropped randomly.

The main question is:

What is the probability that the needle crosses a line?

When L ≤ D, the classic result is

P(crossing) = 2LπD

Use the buttons below to drop 1, 100, or 1000 needles. You can also start an infinite experiment and watch the estimate of π improve.

Red needles cross a line. Dark needles do not cross a line. If the canvas gets crowded, press Reset.

Teaching Notes

  • This is a geometric probability problem.
  • The needle is random in two ways: its position and its angle.
  • Let x be the distance from the center of the needle to the nearest line.
  • Let θ be the angle between the needle and the parallel lines.
  • The needle crosses a line exactly when
    x ≤ L2 sin θ.
  • For many drops, the ratio
    crossings / total drops
    should get close to
    2LπD.

How to estimate π

If you drop the needle N times and it crosses a line C times, then

π ≈ 2LNDC

This estimate becomes better when the number of drops is large.

Move the needle by dragging its center, or adjust the sliders. Change L, D, θ, and x to see exactly when the needle crosses a line.

D means the vertical distance between two neighboring parallel lines. x means the distance from the needle's center to the nearest line.
